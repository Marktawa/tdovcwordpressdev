2021 FSP Allocation for persons with disabilities meeting

11 November 2021

On Thursday the 11th of November 2021, we met as the Disability Peopleâ€™s Organisations Consortium (DPOC) for Kafue District, Lusaka Province, Zambia together with the representative for the Ministry of Community Development and Social Welfare, Leah Mumbi.

The meeting was held to discuss issues surrounding the 2021 FOOD SECURITY PACK (FSP) distribution exercise with respect to the allocation to be given to persons with disabilities.

The FSP is to be given to 2500 vulnerable but viable farmers within Kafue District. FSP which is an Empowerment Program under the Ministry of Community Development and Social Welfare allows a farmer to receive two 50KG bags of Urea, a 50KG bag of D compound, one 10KG bag of Maize seed and a 10KG bag of Legume seed. The full report can be found at the Kafue Times page.

Out of the total FSP, 7.7% is to be allocated to persons with disabilities and families of persons with disabilities.

We commended the government under the leadership of His Excellency President Hakainde Hichilema for this move and pledged to continue working together with the government.

The following resolutions were made from this meeting:

  1. After distribution of farming inputs, the DPOC will monitor all 18 wards to see in person (face to face), the persons with disabilities and their families who are beneficiaries of the FSP.
  2. The DPOC shall work together closely with the Ministry of Community Development and Social Welfare to monitor implementation of this project in the 18 wards of Kafue District.
  3. During the meeting, the representatives for the Ministry of Community Development and Social Welfare in some of the 18 wards assured us that they will make sure the right thing is done..
  4. Each ward with relatively less persons with disabilities are to communicate to the DPOC, so that we allocate the proportionate inputs.
  5. We appreciated the Ministry of Community Development and Social Welfare team for the work they have done so far including registering persons with disability for social cash transfer and hence we are only waiting for the confirmation of the number of beneficiaries to be approved for social cash transfer.
  6. The Ministry of Community Development and Social Welfare also clarified the terms surrounding the fund allocations conditions. The funds are not to be seen as social cash transfer where beneficiaries do not have to pay back. Instead, each beneficiary is supposed to pay back at the end of the harvesting season with 3 bags of grain.
  7. Support in the form of capacity building will be given to the beneficiaries in each ward to ensure success.
  8. We appeal to all councillors in all 18 wards of Kafue District to provide assistance during elections of Ward Development Committees to be inclusive of persons with disabilities including youths with disability to participate in development projects for their respective wards.

The following organisations met as the DPOC:

  • Twatasha Disability Organisation
  • Edmund Rice Care Trust
  • Chikondi Persons Disability
  • Soloboni Ben Therapy
  • Mapalo Disability
  • Young Voice Movement Kafue
  • Bwalo Trust
  • ZNADP
